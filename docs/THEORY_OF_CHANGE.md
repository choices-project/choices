# Theory of change (optional deep read)

_Last updated: April 4, 2026_

This page is for readers who want **why** Choices exists beyond feature lists. It is **not** required to use the product. Default landing and in-app copy stay **procedural**: transparency, participation, and privacy.

## Problem frame (evidence-based)

- **Money in politics** — Campaign finance, lobbying, and disclosure gaps are well-documented; many people across viewpoints want clearer visibility into who funds whom and how that relates to **votes and budgets**.
- **Attention and polarization** — Media and legislative agendas can emphasize symbolic conflict; civic tools can still help people **trace material outcomes** (spending, roll calls, local impacts) without telling anyone their issue is “fake.”
- **Concentration of power** — Two-party dynamics and insider access are mainstream topics in political science; the product’s job is to **surface data**, not to advance a single moral demonology.

## What we believe the product does

1. **Lowers search costs** — Easier paths from “who represents me?” to “what did they fund / vote on?”
2. **Protects sensitive participation** — Strong defaults, opt-in collection, no sale of personal rows.
3. **Supports coordination without tribal capture** — Shared workflows: verify claims, cite sources, localize impact.

## What success is not

- Replacing elections or journalism.
- **Winning** one ideological camp. If only one tribe is comfortable, the data model or copy failed the **trust layer** test.
